Emotional Tips

Talk about your concerns

Negative stereotypes present in the media or in communities can lead to mental health problems that are not okay or not "normal". However, suffering ups and downs is part of the essence of being human and it takes courage to recognize how we feel.


If you are going through a bad time, try talking to someone you trust.

Source

How I can feel better?

  • 1. Plan activities and prioritize among them.
  • 2. Decide between different study techniques.
  • 3. Eating healthy meals on a regular schedule.
  • 4. Practicing sports or recreational activities.
  • 5. Devote time to hobbies and pastimes.
  • 6. Maintain stable sleep cycles.

Source

Recognize your worth

Mental health is a changing process and, whatever point you are at, your value as a person does not change. In order to learn to take care of yourself, it is very important that you accept who you are and how you feel, whether you are sad, feeling great or somewhere in between.

Source

Question your inner critic

We are often our own worst critics and don't hesitate for a second to blame or criticize ourselves. The next time you realize what you are doing, ask yourself the following questions, "Would I talk to a friend like that?", "What would I say if I was having a hard time or had made a mistake?". Treat yourself with the same consideration you would treat anyone else.

Source

Treat yourself well

Nobody is perfect. It is essential to accept ourselves as we are, something that cannot be achieved automatically, but with practice it can be improved. Remember that everyone deserves to be treated kindly and that includes you, no matter how you feel.
